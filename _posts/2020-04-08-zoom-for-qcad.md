With the current COVID-19 pandemic, the Quantum Computing at Davis (QCaD) organization that I work for made the decision 
to host meetings through Zoom. 

The board was very excited with the prospect considering the fact that we now had the ability to reach out to people
beyond the school. 

The option to use Zoom was a bet on the fact that a large pool of students use it and furthermore, Zoom allowed for
screensharing (important for slides/digital whiteboard work) and could extend beyond UC Davis students/staff (a 
limitation of using Hangouts). 

The first meeting went without a hitch (other than me trying to figure out how to keep the chat open so I could
address questions) and the recording has been uploaded to YouTube:

[![](http://img.youtube.com/vi/kyPwAaEnbFA/0.jpg)](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=kyPwAaEnbFA "Winter Quarter 2020 QCaD Meeting 1")

Slides can be found [here](https://docs.google.com/presentation/d/1mj-yoVgmfpVGPnz5l4_6FE1ZiQuK28vMQ-FOB4iHlsE/edit?usp=sharing)

I focused primarily on giving primers on the origins of Quantum Mechanics, what properties of Quantum Mechanics are used 
in Quantum Computing and a distinction between the more universal gate models and "problem-specific" ones such as
Adiabatic Quantum Computation (AQC) and Gaussian Boson Sampling (GBS).

I hope to host many more Zoom meetings in the future focused on Quantum Algorithms, which I feel is a field that 
needs better explanations than the math-dense resources currently available.
